# Spores to Genomics

## Thesis Information
This project investigates the genomic and metagenomic analysis of spores found in archaeological contexts. The focus is on integrating genomic data to better understand ancient microbiomes and their roles in historical environments.

## Research Overview
This research explores the archaeological context of Turku, which reveals a rich historical narrative through its preserved biological remnants. The study aims to establish a link between archaeological findings and genomic insights.

## Sample Information
The study utilizes 9 samples sourced from Itäinen Rantakatu 10, chosen for their relevance to the archaeological site.

## Methodologies
To achieve the research objectives, the following methodologies are employed:
- **Metagenomic Sequencing**: Comprehensive sequencing to capture the diversity of organisms present.
- **Cultivation**: Targeted approaches to grow and identify individual microbial strains.
- **Whole-Genome Sequencing**: In-depth genomic analysis of selected strains to understand their functions and interactions.

## Existing Pipeline Documentation
1. **Sample Collection**: All samples collected were thoroughly documented and preserved.
2. **DNA Extraction**: Procedures for efficient DNA extraction from spore samples.
3. **Sequencing**: Detailed protocols on sequencing methodologies employed.
4. **Data Analysis**: Steps for analyzing the genomic data obtained, including bioinformatics tools utilized.
5. **Interpretation**: Guidelines for interpreting the sequencing results in the context of archaeological findings.

## Conclusion
The integration of archaeological data with genomic analysis offers a unique perspective on historical microbiomes, paving the way for deeper insights into past environments and their inhabitants.
